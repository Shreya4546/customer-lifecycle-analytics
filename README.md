# Marketing Campaign Performance & Customer Insights Analysis: A/B Testing and RFM Segmentation

## Project Overview

This project is an **end-to-end marketing analytics case study** designed to simulate how a data analyst evaluates campaign performance and customer behavior to generate actionable business insights.

The analysis focuses on understanding:

* Marketing campaign effectiveness
* Customer segmentation using **RFM analysis**
* Customer spending behavior across demographics
* Purchase channel preferences
* Conversion performance using **statistical A/B testing**

The project follows a realistic analytics workflow:

**Python → Data Processing & Statistical Testing → Power BI Dashboard**

# Business Questions Addressed

1. Which marketing campaigns generate the highest conversions?
2. Which customer segments contribute the most revenue?
3. How does customer income influence spending behavior?
4. Which purchase channels drive the majority of sales?
5. What is the conversion rate of marketing campaigns?
6. Which customer segments should marketing teams prioritize for retention?

## Project Workflow

The project follows a structured data analytics workflow:

1. Data Cleaning and Preparation using Python
2. Feature Engineering and RFM Segmentation
3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
4. Statistical A/B Testing for Campaign Comparison
5. Business Insight Generation
6. Interactive Dashboard Development in Power BI


# Tech Stack

**Python (Pandas / NumPy / Matplotlib / Seaborn / Statsmodels)**
Used for data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, feature engineering, RFM segmentation, and statistical A/B testing using Z-Test.

**Power BI**
Used for building interactive dashboards, KPI monitoring, DAX measures, campaign performance analysis, and customer behavior visualization.

**Jupyter Notebook**
Used as the development environment for executing Python code, performing analysis, and documenting the analytical workflow.


# Data Model

The dataset contains customer demographic, spending, and marketing campaign interaction data.

Key fields used in the analysis include:

* **Customer ID** – Unique identifier for each customer
* **Annual Income** – Customer income level
* **Total Revenue** – Total amount spent by a customer
* **Total Purchases** – Overall number of purchases made
* **Web Purchases / Store Purchases / Catalog Purchases** – Purchase channel behavior
* **Recency** – Days since last purchase
* **Campaign Acceptance** – Whether the customer accepted a marketing campaign
* **Segment** – Customer segment derived from RFM analysis


# Dashboard & Analysis

## 1. Executive Marketing Overview

This dashboard provides a **high-level overview of marketing campaign performance and customer purchase behavior**.

### Key KPIs

**Total Customers:** 2216

**Total Revenue:** ₹1.35M

**Total Conversions:** 459

**Average Spending:** ₹607.08

These KPIs provide a snapshot of the overall **customer base, revenue generation, and campaign effectiveness**.



## 2. Campaign Performance Analysis

### Campaign Acceptance Comparison

Campaign performance was analyzed by comparing the total conversions generated by each campaign.

**Insights**

* **Campaign 4** achieved the highest conversions (164).
* **Campaign 3 and Campaign 5** also performed well with similar acceptance levels.
* **Campaign 2** performed the worst with only 30 conversions.

This indicates that **Campaign 4's messaging and targeting strategy were significantly more effective**.


### Campaign Conversion Rate

Conversion rate analysis shows how efficiently each campaign converts customers.

**Insights**

* Campaign 4 has the **highest conversion rate (~7.4%)**
* Campaign 3 and Campaign 5 perform similarly
* Campaign 2 has the **lowest conversion rate (~1.4%)**

**Recommendation**

Marketing teams should:

* Increase investment in **Campaign 4 strategy**
* Reevaluate the targeting and messaging strategy for **Campaign 2**


## 3. Purchase Channel Behavior

The analysis compares different purchase channels used by customers.

**Channels analyzed**

* Store Purchases
* Web Purchases
* Catalog Purchases
* Deal Purchases

**Insights**

Store purchases dominate with nearly **39% of total transactions**, indicating that **offline retail channels remain highly influential**.


## 4. Customer Spending Patterns by Age

Customer revenue was analyzed across age groups.

**Insights**

* Customers aged **60+ generate the highest revenue**
* The **46–60 age group** also contributes significantly
* Younger customers show lower spending levels

**Recommendation**

Marketing campaigns should prioritize **high-value older customer segments** with loyalty programs and targeted offers.


# Customer Segmentation Dashboard

## 1. RFM Customer Segmentation

Customers were segmented using **RFM Analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)** to understand behavioral patterns.

Segments identified include:

* Champions
* Loyal Customers
* At Risk
* Potential Loyalists
* Promising / New Customers

**Insights**

* **Champions and Loyal Customers generate the majority of revenue**
* **At Risk customers still contribute a meaningful amount of revenue**
* Retaining high-value segments is critical for revenue stability


## 2. Income vs Spending Analysis

A scatter plot visualizes the relationship between **customer income and total spending**.

**Insights**

* Higher income customers generally contribute higher revenue
* Some lower-income customers still show strong purchasing behavior, indicating **loyal customer groups**


## 3. Campaign Funnel Analysis

The marketing funnel visualizes the conversion journey.

**Funnel Stages**

Contacted Customers → Converted Customers

**Contacted:** 2216
**Converted:** 459

**Overall Conversion Rate:** **20.7%**

**Insight**

Approximately **1 in 5 customers respond positively to marketing campaigns**, indicating a moderately effective marketing strategy.


# Statistical A/B Testing

A statistical **Z-test** was performed in Python to compare campaign acceptance rates and validate whether performance differences between campaigns were statistically significant.

This ensures that campaign decisions are based on **data-driven statistical evidence rather than simple comparison metrics**.


# Key Business Insights

* Campaign 4 is the **best-performing campaign** and should receive higher marketing budget allocation.
* Campaign 2 significantly underperforms and requires strategy redesign.
* **Champions and Loyal customers drive the majority of revenue**, making them critical for retention strategies.
* Store purchases dominate customer behavior, showing the importance of physical retail channels.
* Customers aged **60+ generate the highest spending**, making them a valuable demographic for targeted campaigns.
